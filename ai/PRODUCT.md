# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults on GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) or who have recently completed a GLP-1 course, managing the physiological aftermath of rapid weight loss: muscle degradation, gut integrity disruption, energy and cognitive decline. Split evenly between active-treatment users seeking support now and post-treatment completers rebuilding their baseline.

They arrive skeptical of supplements, burned by overpromising brands. They are health-literate and expect specificity. They've already made a significant commitment (GLP-1 treatment) and are looking for the next responsible step, not a shortcut.

## Product Purpose

AfterBody Health sells two things together: the AfterBody Stack and the AfterBody Protocol.

**The Stack** — three targeted formulas included in every subscription:
- **RESET** — gut integrity and digestive resilience
- **REBUILD** — lean tissue preservation and hormonal support
- **RECHARGE** — cognitive function, energy, and neurochemical stability

**The Protocol** — the system built around the Stack:
- Personalized 90-day nutrition and training program generated from onboarding responses
- Full AfterBody App access: daily macro, workout, body weight, and supplement tracking; leaderboard and community competitions; streak accountability
- Private 30-minute strategy session with Head Nutritionist (Tayler), scheduled after the protocol is ready
- A new protocol delivered every 90 days based on results

The product exists because GLP-1 medications create a specific set of physiological gaps that generic supplements don't address. AfterBody fills exactly those gaps, with transparent dosing and no proprietary blends.

Success is a customer who finishes their minimum commitment period, sees measurable body composition results, and renews.

## Brand Personality

Direct, honest, no-BS. Earns trust through specificity, not enthusiasm. Speaks like a knowledgeable friend who's read the studies, not a brand manager who's polished the pitch. Confident without being aggressive. Clinical without being cold.

## Anti-references

- **Generic supplement brands** (Bodybuilding.com, Optimum Nutrition, GNC): bro-science aesthetic, neon colors, before/after transformation grids, influencer credibility, overclaimed benefits.
- **Wellness soft aesthetics** (Goop, Moon Juice): pastel, airy, ritual-adjacent, spirituality as positioning — AfterBody is science-grounded, not spiritual.
- **Pharma/clinical white**: sterile white backgrounds, navy trust-signals, feels like a waiting room — we're premium, not medical-device.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust through specificity.** Mechanisms, not magic. If a claim can't be backed with a dose or a mechanism, it doesn't belong on the page.
2. **Restraint is the premium signal.** The dark luxe palette, the sparse copy, the deliberate whitespace — these communicate quality. More hype = less trust with this audience.
3. **The body is the hero.** The customer's transformation is what we're selling, not the bottles. Visual hierarchy serves the human outcome.
4. **Direct over diplomatic.** Short sentences. Active voice. No filler. Every word earns its place or gets cut.
5. **Precision as credibility.** Specific doses, named ingredients, transparent labels. Generality is the enemy of trust for a skeptical, health-literate buyer.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Keyboard-navigable interactions, sufficient color contrast against the dark background (gold on dark green must meet 4.5:1 for body text). No motion required for comprehension; animation is additive only.
